## Understanding Commerce Metrics

### Commerce Score

**What it measures**: Overall e-commerce performance combining all commerce-related signals (0-100)

**Factors**:
- Purchase readiness
- ACP signals strength
- Visibility in shopping contexts
- Platform recommendation frequency
- Brand mention strength

**Good score**: 65+
**Needs work**: Below 45

### Purchase Readiness Score

**What it measures**: How close AI responses are to driving an actual purchase (0-100)

**High readiness indicators**:
- Direct purchase recommendations ("You should buy...")
- Clear call-to-action language
- Specific platform mentions
- Positive product endorsements

**Low readiness indicators**:
- Vague or non-committal language
- No platform recommendations
- Cautionary statements
- Negative sentiment

### ACP Signals Score

**What it measures**: AI Commerce Performance - strength of buying recommendations from AI models (0-100)

**Strong signals**:
- "Recommended for purchase"
- "Best choice for..."
- "Top pick"
- High confidence language

**Weak signals**:
- "Could consider"
- "Depends on..."
- Neutral mentions only
- No endorsement

### Brand Mentions

**Visual**: Avatar stack showing logos

**Categories**:
- **Your brand** (highlighted with special styling)
- **Competitors** (known competing brands)
- **Other brands** (additional mentioned brands)

**Interaction**: Hover to see mention counts and recommendation strength

<Tip>
  If competitors appear more frequently in brand mentions, they're capturing more of the purchase intent. Focus on creating content that positions your product as the best choice.
</Tip>

## Improving Commerce Performance

### If Your Commerce Score is Low (Below 45)

<Steps>
  <Step title="Optimize Product Pages">
    Ensure complete, accurate product information on your website
  </Step>
  <Step title="Gather Reviews">
    Accumulate reviews on trusted platforms (Amazon, G2, Trustpilot, etc.)
  </Step>
  <Step title="Clarify Pricing">
    Make pricing transparent and competitive
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add Buying Guides">
    Create content explicitly helping users make purchase decisions
  </Step>
  <Step title="Improve Site Structure">
    Ensure product pages are crawlable and well-organized
  </Step>
</Steps>

### If Your Site Isn't in Top E-Commerce Platforms

<Steps>
  <Step title="Check Site Crawlability">

    Publish "where to buy" and "how to purchase" content
  </Step>
  <Step title="Build Backlinks">
    Get cited by sites AI models trust
  </Step>
</Steps>

## FAQs

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="What's the difference between Commerce and Define pages?">
    **Commerce**: Purchase-focused prompts and e-commerce platform tracking
    **Define**: General brand visibility prompts

    Both track custom prompts you create, but Commerce specializes in shopping queries.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Should I add the same prompts to both Commerce and Define?">
    **No.** Keep them separate:
    - Commerce: "Where can I buy X?", "Should I purchase Y?"
    - Define: "What is X?", "Tell me about Y"

    Different purposes = different tracking
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Why is my Purchase Readiness low but Visibility high?">
    **Cause**: AI knows your brand but doesn't recommend buying it

    **Common reasons**:
    - Negative reviews
    - Lack of product information
    - Stronger competitors
    - Pricing concerns

    **Fix**: Address product quality, reviews, and positioning
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="How often should I add new commerce prompts?">
    **Frequency**: Add 3-5 new prompts monthly

    **Strategy**: Cover different product lines, use cases, and shopping scenarios

    **Total**: Aim for 15-25 commerce prompts total
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
