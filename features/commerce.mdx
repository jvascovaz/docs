## What is Commerce Tracking?

Commerce tracking focuses specifically on purchase-related queries and e-commerce performance:

- **Track purchase intent**: Monitor how often AI models recommend your products
- **Identify e-commerce channels**: See which platforms AI models recommend for purchases
- **Analyze shopping queries**: Understand performance in "where to buy" and "best product" queries
- **Compare shopping platforms**: Benchmark your presence vs. competitors across e-commerce sites
- **Optimize for conversions**: Get insights to improve purchase recommendations

## Creating Commerce Prompts

### Add New Commerce Prompts

Click the **"+ Add Commerce Prompts"** button to create purchase-focused prompts.

  <Step title="Click Add Commerce Prompts">
    Opens the commerce prompt creation interface
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter Prompts">
    Type or paste commerce-focused prompts (one per line)
  </Step>
  <Step title="Get AI Suggestions">

  </Step>

### Commerce Prompt Types

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Direct Purchase">
    Where-to-buy and purchasing queries:
    ```
    "Where can I buy [Product Name]?"
    "How to purchase [Product]"
    "Best place to buy [Product Category]"
    "[Product] for sale"
    "Order [Product] online"
    ```

    **Optimizes For**: Direct e-commerce platform recommendations
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Product Comparisons">
    Competitive shopping queries:
    ```
    "[Your Brand] vs [Competitor] for [use case]"
    "Compare [Product A] and [Product B]"
    "Which is better: [Your Product] or [Competitor Product]?"
    "Difference between [Product A] and [Product B]"
    ```

    **Optimizes For**: Positioning in purchase decisions
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Value Assessment">
    Worth-it and pricing queries:
    ```
    "Is [Product] worth buying?"
    "[Product] worth the price?"
    "Should I buy [Product]?"
    "[Product] value for money"
    "[Product] cost vs benefits"
    ```

    **Optimizes For**: Purchase intent and recommendation strength
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Best-of Queries">
    Category leadership and top picks:
    ```
    "Best [Product Category] to buy"
    "Top [Product Category] for [use case]"
    "[Product Category] recommendations"
    "What [Product Category] should I get?"
    ```

    **Optimizes For**: Category leadership and recommendations
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Shopping Research">
    Pre-purchase research queries:
    ```
    "[Product] reviews before buying"
    "What to know before buying [Product]"
    "[Product] pros and cons"
    "[Product] buying guide"
    "[Product] specifications and features"
    ```

    **Optimizes For**: Informational needs and trust-building
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

### Best Practices for Commerce Prompts

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Use Transactional Language">
    Include purchase-oriented keywords that signal buying intent:
    - **Good**: "Where can I buy noise-canceling headphones?"
    - **Bad**: "Tell me about noise-canceling headphones"

    AI models recognize transactional intent and provide different responses.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Test Platform-Specific Queries">
    Create prompts mentioning specific e-commerce platforms:
    ```
    "Buy [Product] on Amazon"
    "[Product] available on [Platform]?"
    "Find [Product] at [Retailer]"
    ```
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Include Price/Value Prompts">
    Pricing queries often drive high-intent traffic:
    ```
    "[Product] price comparison"
    "Cheapest [Product]"
    "Best value [Product Category]"
    ```
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Cover the Purchase Journey">
    Create prompts for different stages:
    - Research: "What features does [Product] have?"
    - Comparison: "[Product A] vs [Product B]"
    - Decision: "Should I buy [Product]?"
    - Purchase: "Where to buy [Product]?"
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

### Types of Commerce Insights:

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Low Purchase Intent Alerts">
    **What**: Prompts where AI rarely recommends your product

    **Example**: "Only 15% of responses recommend your product for 'best wireless earbuds' queries"

    **Actions**:
    - Improve product positioning in that category
    - Gather more positive reviews
    - Create targeted content highlighting strengths
    - Optimize product pages for that use case
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Missing Platform Opportunities">
    **What**: E-commerce platforms recommended by AI but where you're absent

    **Example**: "AI models recommend Walmart 40% of the time, but your product isn't listed there"

    **Actions**:
    - Expand to recommended platforms
    - Optimize existing but undiscovered listings
    - Partner with recommended retailers
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Competitor E-Commerce Advantages">
    **What**: Platforms where competitors dominate recommendations

    **Example**: "Competitor X appears 60% more on Amazon recommendations"

    **Actions**:
    - Investigate competitor Amazon strategy
    - Improve your Amazon presence
    - Differentiate on platforms where you're stronger
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Pricing Perception Issues">
    **What**: AI models cite price as a concern

    **Example**: "30% of responses mention your product is expensive compared to alternatives"

    **Actions**:
    - Justify pricing with value propositions
    - Highlight unique features
    - Create bundles or promotions
    - Position against premium competitors, not budget options
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Review & Rating Gaps">
    **What**: Competitors have better review signals

    **Example**: "Competitors average 4.5 stars vs your 4.0 - impacting recommendations"

    **Actions**:
    - Focus on gathering more positive reviews
    - Address common complaints
    - Improve product quality
    - Optimize review solicitation process
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Commerce Metrics Explained

### Purchase Intent Strength

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Strong (80-100)">
    **Indicators**:
    - "Highly recommended"
    - "Best choice for"
    - "Top pick"
    - Explicit purchase recommendations

    **Example**: "For noise cancellation, Sony WH-1000XM5 is the top choice and highly recommended."
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Moderate (50-79)">
    **Indicators**:
    - Listed among options
    - Conditional recommendations
    - "Good option for"
    - Included in comparisons

    **Example**: "Sony WH-1000XM5 is a good option for travelers, though also consider Bose QC45."
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Weak (20-49)">
    **Indicators**:
    - Mentioned but not recommended
    - Lower in ranked lists
    - Qualified with caveats
    - Alternative suggestions prioritized

    **Example**: "While Sony WH-1000XM5 exists, Bose QC45 offers better value."
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="None (0-19)">
    **Indicators**:
    - Product not mentioned
    - Competitors exclusively recommended
    - Brand mentioned but products not suggested

    **Example**: "For wireless headphones, consider Bose QC45 or Apple AirPods Max."
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

### Platform Recommendation Rate

<ParamField path="Definition" type="string">
  Percentage of commerce prompts where a specific platform is recommended
</ParamField>

<ParamField path="Calculation" type="formula">
  (Prompts recommending platform / Total commerce prompts) × 100
</ParamField>

<ParamField path="Good Score" type="number">
  - Your own site: 50%+
  - Major marketplaces: 30%+ (if you sell there)
  - Specialized retailers: 20%+ (industry-dependent)
</ParamField>

### Average Position in Recommendations

<ParamField path="Definition" type="string">
  Average ranking when your product appears in shopping lists
</ParamField>

<ParamField path="Good Score" type="number">
  - Position 1-2: Excellent (preferred recommendation)
  - Position 3-5: Good (solid consideration)
  - Position 6-10: Fair (needs improvement)
  - Position 10+: Poor (optimization required)
</ParamField>

## Commerce Optimization Strategies

### 1. Own Your Direct Channel

<Steps>
  <Step title="Optimize Product Pages">
    - Add comprehensive product descriptions
    - Include high-quality images and videos
    - Display customer reviews prominently
    - Use schema markup for rich snippets
  </Step>
  <Step title="Improve Site Authority">
    - Build backlinks to product pages
    - Get featured in product review sites
    - Appear in buying guides
    - Gather external validation
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enhance Trust Signals">
    - Display security badges
    - Show money-back guarantees
    - Highlight shipping/return policies
    - Feature customer testimonials
  </Step>
</Steps>

### 2. Dominate Marketplace Platforms

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Amazon Optimization" icon="amazon">
    - Enhanced Brand Content (A+ Content)
    - Bullet point optimization
    - Backend keyword targeting
    - Amazon Vine reviews
  </Card>
  <Card title="Walmart Marketplace" icon="store">
    - Complete Rich Media content
    - Competitive pricing
    - Fast fulfillment options
    - Pro Seller Badge
  </Card>
  <Card title="Specialized Platforms" icon="tags">
    - Industry-specific optimizations
    - Category expertise demonstration
    - Platform-specific promotions
  </Card>
  <Card title="Shopify/DTC" icon="cart-shopping">
    - SEO-optimized product pages
    - Customer review integration
    - Social proof widgets
    - Content marketing
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

### 3. Build Review Excellence

<Info>
  Reviews are critical signals AI models use for purchase recommendations. Prioritize gathering high-quality, authentic reviews.
</Info>

**Review Strategy**:
- Solicit reviews from happy customers (within platform guidelines)
- Respond to all reviews (positive and negative)
- Address common complaints in product improvements
- Highlight positive reviews in marketing
- Maintain 4.5+ average rating when possible

### 4. Competitive Pricing Strategy

<Warning>
  AI models often consider value for money. Being "expensive" without clear justification hurts recommendations.
</Warning>

**Pricing Options**:
- **Match competitors**: For parity products
- **Premium positioning**: Justify with unique features/quality
- **Value positioning**: Highlight features per dollar
- **Bundle strategies**: Create favorable comparison points

## Troubleshooting

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Product Not Recommended">
    **Possible Causes**:
    - Insufficient reviews/ratings
    - Poor SEO/discoverability
    - Price not competitive
    - Negative sentiment

    **Solutions**:
    - Focus on review generation
    - Improve product page optimization
    - Adjust pricing or justify premium positioning
    - Address quality/service issues
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Wrong Platform Recommended">
    **Example**: AI recommends Amazon but you want to drive direct sales

    **Solutions**:
    - Strengthen direct site presence in search results
    - Create exclusive direct-only offers
    - Build brand authority outside marketplaces
    - Optimize direct site for AI citation
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Competitor Always Wins Comparisons">
    **Causes**: Better reviews, lower price, stronger authority, or clearer differentiation

    **Solutions**:
    - Identify specific advantages competitors have
    - Match on table-stakes features
    - Differentiate on unique strengths
    - Target different use cases or audiences
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Low Purchase Intent Despite High Visibility">
    **Cause**: Brand awareness exists but trust/recommendation signals are weak

    **Solutions**:
    - Focus on review quality and quantity
    - Build authority through press and recognition
    - Improve product positioning and messaging
    - Highlight social proof and credibility signals
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
