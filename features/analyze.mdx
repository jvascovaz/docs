## The Four Core Metrics

### 1. Visibility

**What it measures**: How often your brand appears in AI-generated responses

**Data source**: System prompts created during onboarding (~25 prompts)

**Sub-metrics**:
- Mention frequency
- Position in responses (first, top 3, average)
- Template coverage (% of prompt types where you appear)
- Consistency across AI models
- Share of voice (your mentions vs total)

**Good score**: 70+
**Needs work**: Below 50

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="How to Improve Visibility">
    <Steps>
      <Step title="Create Comprehensive Content">
        Publish authoritative content covering key topics in your category
      </Step>
      <Step title="Get High-Quality Backlinks">
        Earn citations from sources AI models trust (Wikipedia, major publications, industry sites)
      </Step>
      <Step title="Optimize Website Structure">
        Ensure your site is well-organized with clear navigation and proper schema markup
      </Step>
      <Step title="Build Category Authority">
        Establish thought leadership through guest posts, speaking engagements, and industry contributions
      </Step>
    </Steps>
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

### 2. Authority

**What it measures**: Recognition as a category leader or trusted expert

**Data source**: System prompts analyzing expertise and trust signals

**Sub-metrics**:
- Source quality (tier of sites citing you)
- Expertise recognition (how often AI calls you an expert)
- Citation context (authoritative vs. passing mention)
- Category leadership positioning

**Good score**: 65+
**Needs work**: Below 45

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="How to Improve Authority">
    <Steps>
      <Step title="Build High-Authority Backlinks">
        Get cited by Wikipedia, major publications, and industry-leading sites
      </Step>
      <Step title="Publish Original Research">
        Create data-driven reports and studies that others cite
      </Step>
      <Step title="Earn Industry Recognition">
        Apply for awards, get analyst coverage (Gartner, Forrester)
      </Step>
      <Step title="Contribute Expert Content">
        Write for authoritative publications, speak at conferences
      </Step>
    </Steps>
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

### 3. Sentiment

**What it measures**: How positively or negatively AI models discuss your brand

**Data source**: Sentiment analysis of all brand mentions in system prompt responses

**Scale**:
- **70-100**: Positive sentiment (praise, recommendations)
- **40-69**: Neutral/Mixed sentiment (factual mentions)
- **0-39**: Negative sentiment (criticism, warnings)

**Good score**: 65+
**Needs work**: Below 50 or trending downward

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="How to Improve Sentiment">
    <Steps>
      <Step title="Address Root Causes">
        Fix actual product/service issues driving negative feedback
      </Step>
      <Step title="Gather Positive Reviews">
        Encourage satisfied customers to leave reviews on trusted platforms
      </Step>
      <Step title="Publish Success Stories">
        Create case studies and customer testimonials
      </Step>
      <Step title="Manage Reputation">
        Respond professionally to criticism and demonstrate continuous improvement
      </Step>
    </Steps>
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

### 4. Purchase Intent

**What it measures**: Whether AI models recommend your brand for purchase decisions

**Data source**: Analysis of recommendation strength in system prompt responses

**Sub-metrics**:
- Recommendation strength (how strongly AI suggests you)
- Purchase language (presence of "should buy", "best for", "recommended")
- Comparison positioning (where you rank vs competitors)
- Use case matching (alignment with user needs)

**Good score**: 60+
**Needs work**: Below 40

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="How to Improve Purchase Intent">
    <Steps>
      <Step title="Optimize Product Information">
        Ensure complete, accurate product details on your website
      </Step>
      <Step title="Gather Reviews & Ratings">
        Accumulate positive reviews on G2, Capterra, Trustpilot, Amazon, etc.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Clarify Use Cases">
        Create content explicitly matching products to specific user needs
      </Step>
      <Step title="Show Social Proof">
        Display customer counts, success metrics, testimonials
      </Step>
    </Steps>
  </Accordion>

## Using the Charts

### Metric Trend Chart (Left)

Shows your selected metric's score over time.

**What you'll see**:
- Line chart with data points
- X-axis: Time (dates)
- Y-axis: Score (0-100)
- Hover tooltips with exact values

**Use cases**:
- Identify upward or downward trends
- Spot sudden changes
- Correlate score changes with your marketing activities

### Subcomponent Trend Chart (Right)

Shows the individual sub-metrics that combine to create the overall score.

**What you'll see**:
- Multiple lines (one per sub-metric)
- Same time period as left chart
- Different colored lines for each component
- Legend showing which line is which

**Use cases**:
- Understand what's driving overall score changes
- Identify which sub-metric needs attention
- See if improvements in one area are offset by declines in another

<Tip>
  If your overall score is flat but you see diverging sub-metric lines, it means improvements in some areas are being offset by declines in others. Focus on the declining components.
</Tip>

## Score Breakdown

Click to expand the breakdown and see exactly how your score is calculated.


**What you'll see**:
- List of all sub-metrics
- Each sub-metric's individual score
- Weight percentage (how much it contributes)
- Weighted contribution to final score
- Description of what each sub-metric measures

**Example for Visibility**:
- Mention Frequency: 85 Ã— 30% weight = 25.5 points
- Position Quality: 72 Ã— 25% weight = 18.0 points
- Template Coverage: 68 Ã— 20% weight = 13.6 points
- Consistency: 90 Ã— 15% weight = 13.5 points
- Share of Voice: 55 Ã— 10% weight = 5.5 points

**Total Visibility Score**: 76.1 / 100

<Info>
  Focus optimization efforts on high-weight components for maximum impact. A 10-point improvement in a 30% weighted component adds 3 points to your overall score, while the same improvement in a 10% weighted component only adds 1 point.
</Info>

## Data Source

<Note>
  **Important**: The Analyze page shows metrics calculated from **system-generated prompts** (~25 prompts created during onboarding Phase 4).

  These prompts cover standard brand health queries and are different from the user-created prompts you see on the Define page.

  **Why separate?**
  - System prompts provide consistent, standardized brand health measurement
  - User prompts track custom queries you specifically care about
  - Both are valuable but serve different purposes
</Note>

## Best Practices

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Monitor Weekly">
    **Frequency**: Check Analyze page once per week

    **What to do**:
    - Review overall brand health score
    - Scan for significant trend changes (>10% movement)
    - Read new insights in the panel
    - Note any competitive alerts

    **Time required**: 5-10 minutes
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Deep Dive Monthly">
    **Frequency**: Monthly detailed analysis

    **What to do**:
    - Review each metric's detailed breakdown
    - Compare 30-day vs 90-day trends
    - Identify patterns and correlations
    - Plan optimization initiatives based on insights

    **Time required**: 30-45 minutes
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Strategic Review Quarterly">
    **Frequency**: Quarterly business review

    **What to do**:
    - Analyze 90-day and 180-day trends
    - Benchmark against competitors
    - Assess ROI of AEO initiatives
    - Set strategic goals for next quarter

    **Time required**: 1-2 hours
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Focus on One Metric at a Time">
    **Why**: Diluting efforts across all 4 metrics reduces impact

    **Strategy**:
    1. Pick your priority metric based on business goals
    2. Run a focused 30-day improvement campaign
    3. Measure results
    4. Move to next priority metric

    **Example**: E-commerce brand focuses on Purchase Intent first, then Sentiment
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Set Realistic Goals">
    **Short-term (1-3 months)**: 5-10 point improvements
    **Medium-term (3-6 months)**: 15-20 point improvements
    **Long-term (6-12 months)**: 25+ point improvements

    **Note**: Diminishing returns apply - moving from 40 to 60 is easier than 80 to 90
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Troubleshooting

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="No data showing / Loading skeleton">
    **Cause**: Analysis not yet complete for this brand

    **Solution**: Wait 2-5 minutes after completing onboarding. System prompts are analyzed automatically in the background.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Scores seem too low/high">
    **Cause**: Limited data points or unusual sample

    **Check**:
    - Are system prompts being analyzed regularly?
    - Is your brand name spelled correctly in brand settings?
    - Are competitors set up correctly?

    **Solution**: Contact support if scores seem incorrect after verifying setup
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Charts not updating after period change">
    **Cause**: UI refresh issue

    **Solution**: Refresh the page. If issue persists, clear browser cache.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Missing insights for a metric">
    **Cause**: Not enough data or no actionable patterns detected

    **Solution**: This is normal - insights only appear when AI identifies specific, actionable recommendations
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
