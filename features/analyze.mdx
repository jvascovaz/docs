## What is Analyze?

Analyze helps you:

- **Track four core metrics**: Visibility, Sentiment, Authority, and Purchase Intent
- **See detailed breakdowns**: Understand what drives each metric score
- **Compare AI models**: See performance across GPT-4, Claude, Gemini, etc.
- **Monitor trends over time**: Track improvements or declines
- **Get actionable insights**: AI-powered recommendations for each metric

## The Four Core Metrics

### 1. Visibility

Measures how frequently and prominently your brand appears in AI-generated responses.

<Frame>
  <img
    src="/images/analyze-visibility-metric.png"
    alt="Visibility Metric"
    style={{ borderRadius: '12px' }}
  />
</Frame>

#### What Visibility Includes:

<ParamField path="Mention Frequency" type="number">
  How often your brand is mentioned across all prompts (0-100 scale)
</ParamField>

<ParamField path="Position Analysis" type="object">
  Where your brand appears in responses:
  - First position rate
  - Top 3 position rate
  - Average position overall
</ParamField>

<ParamField path="Template Coverage" type="number">
  Percentage of different prompt types where your brand appears
</ParamField>

<ParamField path="Consistency Score" type="number">
  How reliably your brand is mentioned across different AI models
</ParamField>

<ParamField path="Share of Voice" type="percentage">
  Your brand mentions vs total mentions in your category
</ParamField>

#### Improving Visibility:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Create Targeted Content">
    Publish comprehensive content covering key prompts where you have low visibility
  </Step>
  <Step title="Optimize for AI Citation">
    Ensure your website is well-structured, authoritative, and cited by high-quality sources
  </Step>
  <Step title="Build Category Authority">
    Establish thought leadership through guest posts, speaking, and industry contributions
  </Step>
  <Step title="Monitor Competitors">
    Study why competitors appear more frequently and replicate their successful strategies
  </Step>
</Steps>

### 2. Sentiment

Tracks the emotional tone and context surrounding your brand mentions.

<Frame>
  <img
    src="/images/analyze-sentiment-metric.png"
    alt="Sentiment Metric"
    style={{ borderRadius: '12px' }}
  />
</Frame>

#### Sentiment Components:

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Positive Sentiment (70-100)">
    **Indicators**:
    - Recommendations and praise
    - "Best choice" or "top pick" language
    - Positive adjectives and endorsements
    - Success stories and customer satisfaction

    **Example**: "Brand X is highly recommended for its excellent customer service and innovative features."
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Neutral Sentiment (40-69)">
    **Indicators**:
    - Factual mentions without opinion
    - List inclusions without preference
    - Balanced comparisons
    - Descriptive but not evaluative

    **Example**: "Brand X offers project management features including task tracking and team collaboration."
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Negative Sentiment (0-39)">
    **Indicators**:
    - Criticisms or warnings
    - "Not recommended" language
    - Negative comparisons
    - Problem mentions

    **Example**: "Brand X has been criticized for poor customer support and high pricing."
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

#### Improving Sentiment:

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Address Root Causes" icon="wrench">
    Fix actual product/service issues driving negative sentiment
  </Card>
  <Card title="Gather Positive Reviews" icon="star">
    Encourage satisfied customers to leave reviews on trusted platforms
  </Card>
  <Card title="Create Positive Content" icon="pen">
    Publish case studies, success stories, and positive customer testimonials
  </Card>
  <Card title="Manage Reputation" icon="shield">
    Respond to criticism professionally and demonstrate improvement
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

### 3. Authority

Measures recognition as a category leader or trusted source.

<Frame>
  <img
    src="/images/analyze-authority-metric.png"
    alt="Authority Metric"
    style={{ borderRadius: '12px' }}
  />

#### Authority Signals:

<ParamField path="Source Quality" type="object">
  Quality and authority of sources citing your brand:
  - Tier 1 sources (Wikipedia, major publications)
  - Tier 2 sources (industry sites, reputable blogs)
  - Tier 3 sources (general websites)
</ParamField>

<ParamField path="Expertise Recognition" type="number">
  How often AI models identify your brand as an expert or leader
</ParamField>

<ParamField path="Citation Context" type="string">
  Context in which your brand is cited (authoritative vs. passing mention)
</ParamField>

<ParamField path="Category Leadership" type="boolean">
  Whether AI models position you as a category leader
</ParamField>

#### Building Authority:

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Content Strategy">
    - Publish in-depth, authoritative content
    - Create original research and data
    - Develop comprehensive guides and resources
    - Maintain a consistent thought leadership voice
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Backlink Building">
    - Get cited by high-authority publications
    - Contribute to industry publications
    - Appear on podcasts and webinars
    - Sponsor or speak at industry events
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Wikipedia & Knowledge Bases">
    - Ensure accurate Wikipedia presence (if notable)
    - Claim and optimize knowledge graph entities
    - Maintain accurate business listings
    - Contribute to industry wikis and resources
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Awards & Recognition">
    - Apply for industry awards
    - Seek analyst recognition (Gartner, Forrester)
    - Earn certifications and badges
    - Highlight customer success stories
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

<Frame>

#### Purchase Intent Factors:

<ParamField path="Recommendation Strength" type="number">

</ParamField>

<Steps>

  </Step>
  <Step title="Clarify Use Cases">
    Create content explicitly matching your products to specific user needs
  </Step>
  <Step title="Highlight Differentiators">
    Clearly communicate unique value propositions and competitive advantages
  </Step>
  <Step title="Show Social Proof">
    Display customer counts, success metrics, and case studies
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Why Model Performance Varies:

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Training Data Differences">
    Each AI model is trained on different datasets with varying cutoff dates. Your brand may appear more in some training sets than others.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Source Citation Preferences">
    Different models trust different sources. GPT-4 might cite Wikipedia more, while Claude prefers academic sources.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Response Style Variations">
    Models have different personalities and response patterns. Some are more likely to make recommendations.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Recency Bias">
    Models with more recent training data may reflect current brand positioning better than older models.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

### Optimizing for Specific Models:

<Tip>
  If a particular AI model is critical for your audience (e.g., enterprise buyers use Claude), prioritize optimizing sources that model trusts.
</Tip>

## Best Practices

### Monitoring Cadence

<Steps>
  <Step title="Weekly Check-ins">
    Review trend charts and overall brand health score. Look for sudden changes or anomalies.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Monthly Deep Dives">
    Analyze each metric in detail, review insights, and plan optimization initiatives.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Quarterly Strategic Reviews">
    Assess long-term trends (90-day view), benchmark competitors, and set strategic goals.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Setting Realistic Goals

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Short-Term (1-3 months)" icon="calendar">
    Aim for 5-10 point improvements in individual metrics through tactical optimizations
  </Card>
  <Card title="Medium-Term (3-6 months)" icon="calendar-days">
    Target 15-20 point improvements through sustained content and authority building
  </Card>
  <Card title="Long-Term (6-12 months)" icon="calendar-range">
    Strive for 25+ point improvements with comprehensive AEO strategy

  <Card title="Competitive Parity (varies)" icon="users">

### Prioritizing Metrics

Not all metrics matter equally for every brand:

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="E-Commerce Brands">
    **Priority**: Purchase Intent > Sentiment > Visibility > Authority

    **Reasoning**: Driving conversions is the primary goal
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="B2B SaaS">
    **Priority**: Authority > Purchase Intent > Visibility > Sentiment

    **Reasoning**: Trust and expertise drive B2B decisions
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Consumer Brands">
    **Priority**: Sentiment > Visibility > Purchase Intent > Authority

    **Reasoning**: Brand perception and awareness drive consumer choice
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Professional Services">
    **Priority**: Authority > Sentiment > Visibility > Purchase Intent

    **Reasoning**: Expertise and reputation are critical for services
  </Accordion>

## Troubleshooting

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Scores Not Updating">
    **Cause**: No recent prompt executions or data refresh needed

    **Solution**: Execute prompts from the Define page to generate fresh data
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Missing Data for Specific Metric">
    **Cause**: Insufficient prompt coverage for that metric type

    **Solution**: Create more prompts targeting that metric (e.g., purchase-focused prompts for Purchase Intent)
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Inconsistent Scores Across Models">
    **Cause**: Different training data and source preferences

    **Solution**: This is normal - optimize for the models most important to your audience
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Sudden Score Drop">
    **Causes**: Competitor improvement, negative PR, AI model update, or technical issue

    **Solution**: Check competitor activity, recent news, AI model changelog, and re-execute prompts
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
